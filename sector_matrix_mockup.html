Sectoral Comparison Matrix
This panel tracks the structural mismatch between where economic value accrues and where employment concentrates. The framework identifies this divergence as a core symptom of domestic governance failure: an economy where earnings concentrate in tech while jobs grow primarily in cost-inflated healthcare indicates structural misalignment, not healthy specialization.
What to watch: Employment growing while productivity stagnates (healthcare pathology). Output growing while employment declines (automation pattern). Unfilled openings rising while wages stagnate (skills mismatch).

Sector Employment i
Employment (CES, Monthly)
All employees by sector from BLS Current Employment Statistics. Monthly, seasonally adjusted, in thousands. Delta shows 12-month change.
Avg Weekly Wage i
Average Weekly Wages (QCEW, Quarterly)
BLS Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages. Lags by ~6 months. Used as a proxy for median wages, which are only available annually. Watch for sectors where wages stagnate despite openings — a structural skills mismatch signal.
Openings i
Job Openings (JOLTS, Monthly)
Unfilled positions by sector from JOLTS. Monthly, in thousands, ~2 month lag. Rising openings with stagnant employment = skills mismatch. Rising openings with rising wages = genuine labor scarcity.
Output Index i
Output / Production Index
Industrial Production for manufacturing (monthly, FRED). BEA value-added for services sectors (quarterly). Differing frequencies noted per sector. Output growing faster than employment = productivity gains or automation.
